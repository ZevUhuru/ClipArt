# Session — 2026-04-28

## Theme

Clipart-first strategy, bundles, and the character sheet opportunity.

## Conversation Summary

The strategic focus moved from "AI image generator" toward one clear product wedge:

> The fastest way to find, generate, and download reusable transparent clip art for real projects.

We discussed that clip.art is already showing early SEO promise, including ranking near the top for `AI Clipart`, rapidly growing indexed pages, and preparing an ESY-powered automation pipeline that can produce reviewed clip art inventory every day.

## Business Direction

### Credits before subscriptions

Current recommendation:

- Keep credit packs for now.
- Do not rush subscriptions until repeat-use patterns are clearer.
- Use credits to learn conversion, trust, generation frequency, and purchase intent.
- Add subscriptions later once users treat clip.art as a weekly tool.

The current credit ladder can stay generous while the site is learning. Pricing is less important right now than proving that visitors trust transparent, reusable outputs enough to buy.

### MRR expectations

We framed `$10k MRR` as a very feasible serious target if SEO momentum and quality continue.

Potential paths:

- `$1k-$3k MRR`: near-term validation target.
- `$10k-$25k MRR`: realistic 6-12 month target with category depth, transparent PNG trust, and bundles.
- `$50k-$100k+ MRR`: possible if clip.art becomes the default modern clip art catalog and bundle marketplace.

Key formula:

> indexed useful pages x conversion trust x repeat use = MRR

## Bundle Strategy

The core insight:

> Bundles may be the real monetization wedge.

Legacy clip art sites and search results often present scattered individual assets. Marketplaces like Etsy, Design Bundles, and Teachers Pay Teachers prove that creators buy coordinated sets.

clip.art should do both:

- Lots of individual clip art pages for search coverage.
- Thousands of useful theme packs / bundles for project-level value.

Example:

Instead of `/flower` only showing scattered flower clip art, it should eventually expose:

- Rose clip art packs
- Sunflower clip art packs
- Wildflower classroom packs
- Wedding flower PNG bundles
- Spring flower sticker packs
- Floral border and corner sets
- Flower character and mascot packs

This creates a stronger user experience than a generic grid and gives Google richer, more useful landing pages.

## Character Sheet Insight

We identified character sheets as a high-value extension of clip art.

Character sheets are effectively:

> Structured clip art packs for AI video and consistent character workflows.

The pain:

- AI video characters drift.
- Creators need the same character across poses, angles, expressions, and scenes.
- A character sheet gives them a reusable reference set.

This is closely aligned with clip.art because character sheets are:

- Reusable visual assets.
- Often isolated or transparent.
- Packaged as coordinated sets.
- Useful in AI video, storyboards, games, classrooms, mascots, and brand content.

## Pricing Takeaway For Character Sheets

Do not give the full value away for free.

Recommended approach:

- Use free samples for SEO and trust.
- Charge for full transparent PNG packs and complete pose/expression sets.

Possible pricing:

- Free sample: one simple limited character.
- Starter: `$4.99-$9.99`, one character, 6-8 poses.
- Standard: `$14.99-$24.99`, one character, 12-20 assets.
- Premium: `$39-$79`, multi-character sets.
- Custom reviewed character sheet: `$19-$49`.

## Product Takeaway

The vision is not only "generate an image." It is:

> Manufacture useful, indexed, reusable visual inventory and package it into project-ready sets.

Individual clip art pages win long-tail SEO.

Bundles win perceived value, purchasing intent, and differentiation.

Character sheet packs may become one of the highest-value bundle types because they solve a new, urgent AI video workflow problem.
